Ship Specifications

To set up a ship, players distribute exactly 30 units among the following specs. Each spec defines the fundamental capabilities of your vessel:

Attack 1 - 10
Determines how far the ship can fire its main weapons.
Shield 1 - 10
The maximum energy value that shields can absorb before taking hull damage.
Distance 1 - 10
The maximum number of units (hexes) the ship can travel in a single turn.
Resistance 1 - 10
The hull integrity of the ship, which absorbs damage after shields fail.
Comms 1 - 10
The maximum range at which the ship can establish communications.
Maneuver 3, 6, 10
Allows in-place rotation movements on top of distance.

Turn Sequence

Every turn, a player rolls two 6-sided dice (2d6). The sum of the rolled numbers represents the points they can temporarily distribute among their active turn variables:

  • Distance Adjust current speed. Each 1 point spent increases or decreases speed by 1, up to the ship's maximum spec.
  • Attack Set the active attack range and damage potential for the current turn (cannot exceed local Attack spec).
  • Shield Recover damaged shield points at a cost of 1 allocated point per 1 shield point recovered (up to Shield spec).
  • Maneuver Allocate rotation points for the turn (cannot exceed Maneuver spec level).
  • Comms Set active communication range for the turn (cannot exceed Comms spec).
Constraint: None of the turn-allocated variables can surpass their maximum values defined by your Ship Specs.

Movements

Navigating the cold void requires precise speed calculations:

  • 🚀 Fixed movement: Ships must move the exact Distance value currently set (no more, no less).
  • 🔄 Mid-travel rotation: At any point during movement, the player can spend Maneuver points allocated for the turn to rotate the ship in place (e.g., 60 degrees per point on a hex grid), and then continue moving if movement points remain.
  • 💾 Conservation: The speed/distance points set are preserved for the next turn unless actively adjusted.

Combat Mechanics

Engage target vessels using straight-line attacks from your forward-facing firing arc:

Range Requirement: Attacks can only be made in a straight line from the ship's firing arc (forward-facing).

If a target is within your active Attack range, it takes damage calculated as follows:

Damage = 1 + (Attack Points Allocated) - (Distance to Target)

Damage points are first used to reduce the target's Shield. Any remaining damage is subtracted from the target's Resistance (hull). A ship is destroyed when its Resistance reaches 0.

Double Dice Events

Rolling matching pairs on your 2d6 turn roll triggers special galaxy-wide anomalies or events:

⚅⚅
Double Sixes (12)
The player can move the Space Station up to 6 units.
⚀⚀
Double Ones (2)
One of the other players (the one who rolls the highest) can move the Space Storm up to 3 hexes in any direction.
⚂⚂
Other Doubles
The active player can change resources on any one planet.

Trading & Theft

Accumulate wealth and transport commodities across space stations and planets:

Trading Protocols

Trading can happen in three distinct ways:

  • 1 When both ships land on the same Planet.
  • 2 When both ships land in the same Space Station.
  • 3 When both ships are within their active Comms Range.

Thievery

👤 Planetary Theft: When a ship is at a planet, it can steal cargo from another ship docked at that same planet.

Planets & Space Stations

The stellar bodies and installations scattered throughout the sector:

Planets

Capacity: Unlimited ships
  • Trade cargo between docked ships
  • Sell cargo
  • Buy cargo
  • Repair ship hull integrity (Resistance)
  • Accept pending missions

Space Stations

Capacity: Max 2 ships at a time
  • Trade cargo between docked ships
  • Sell specialized cargo
  • Repair ship hull integrity (Resistance)
  • Accept pending missions
  • Refit or Change ship

Missions

Earn credits and reputational standing by assisting planetary governments and orbital corporations:

📋 Mission Lifecycle: Missions are offered by orbital stations and must be accepted while docked at planets.

Standard missions consist of:

  • Picking up specific cargo packages at one planet.
  • Delivering the cargo securely to another designated planet.
